Longhorn Hall of Fame

A short interactive walkthrough of the defining moments in Texas football history.

Click “Start” to enter. Then use the arrows to move through the hall.

1942: Rose Bowl Win

One of the earliest national-stage moments: Texas wins the Rose Bowl, planting the flag as a major program.

1963: National Champions

An undefeated season that defined an era and made Texas a national standard.

1969: “Game of the Century” Season

Another undefeated run and a season remembered as an all-time classic—Texas is on top again.

1970: Another National Title

Proof the peak wasn’t a fluke: Texas closes out the Royal era’s dominance with another championship.

1977: Earl Campbell Wins the Heisman

A legendary individual peak: the “Tyler Rose” becomes Texas’ first Heisman Trophy winner.

1996: Big 12 Champions

A new conference, a new statement: Texas wins the Big 12 and kicks off a new era of national relevance.

1998: Ricky Williams Wins the Heisman

Another program-defining icon: a second Heisman winner and one of the most famous moments in Longhorn history.

2005: National Champions

The modern signature moment: a title season capped by one of the most famous championship games ever.

2023–2024: Big 12 Farewell + SEC Move

Texas wins the Big 12 Championship in its final year, then begins a new era by joining the SEC.
